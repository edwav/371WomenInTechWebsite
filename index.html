Women that changed the tech world

Discover the trailblazing women who have made significant contributions to technology.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Ada Lovelace (1815-1852)

Time Period: Mid-19th Century

Famous For: First Computer Programmer

Achievements: Ada Lovelace's most notable contribution was her work on Charles Babbage's Analytical Engine. She wrote the first algorithm intended to be processed by a machine. Lovelace's foresight into the capabilities of computers extended beyond mere number crunching; she envisioned a future where machines could create music and art, essentially predicting the field of computer programming and its applications in various creative domains.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Grace Hopper (1906-1992)

Time Period: 20th Century

Famous For: Pioneer in Computer Science

Achievements: Grace Hopper's groundbreaking work led to the development of COBOL, one of the first high-level programming languages. She conceptualized the idea of machine-independent programming languages, which led to the creation of COBOL. Hopper is also famous for coining the term "computer bug" after discovering a moth causing malfunctions in the Harvard Mark II computer, reflecting her hands-on approach and problem-solving skills in the early days of computing.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Hedy Lamarr (1914-2000)

Time Period: Mid-20th Century

Famous For: Inventor and Actress

Achievements: Hedy Lamarr, in collaboration with composer George Antheil, invented a frequency-hopping communication system initially designed to guide torpedoes without detection or interference during World War II. Although the U.S. Navy did not adopt the technology until later, her invention laid the groundwork for modern wireless communications technologies, including Bluetooth, GPS, and Wi-Fi, showcasing her innovative spirit and contribution to wireless communication technology.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Katherine Johnson (1918-2020)

Time Period: Mid-20th Century

Famous For: NASA Mathematician

Achievements: Katherine Johnson's expertise in trajectory analysis was vital for NASA's first US space flight. Her complex manual calculations supported numerous space missions, including John Glenn's orbit around Earth. Glenn specifically requested Johnson's verification of the calculations before his flight. In recognition of her monumental contributions, she received the Presidential Medal of Freedom at age 97.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Betty Snyder (1921-2011)

Time Period: Mid-20th Century

Famous For: Computer Graphics Pioneer

Achievements: Betty Snyder was instrumental in the development of the UNIVAC computer, one of the first commercially successful computers. Her work included creating the first computer graphics program, significantly contributing to the fields of computer graphics and visualization. Her innovations have had a lasting impact on how data and information are visually represented in computing.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Jean Bartik (1924-2011)

Time Period: Mid-20th Century

Famous For: Original ENIAC Programmer

Achievements: Jean Bartik was one of the six original programmers of ENIAC, the first general-purpose electronic digital computer. Her work involved developing software for the machine before software engineering was formally recognized as a field. Bartik's contributions were crucial in the transition from the mechanical era to the electronic era of computing, but her work was not widely recognized until many years later.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Elizabeth Feinler (born 1931)

Time Period: Late 20th Century

Famous For: The Original Search Engine

Achievements: Elizabeth Feinler ran the Network Information Center, which functioned like an early form of Google from 1972 to 1989. She led the development of the first internet directories, including the original "white pages" and “yellow pages”. Her team also established the domain naming scheme (.com, .edu, .gov, .net, etc.) that is ubiquitous in today's internet infrastructure.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Frances E. Allen (1932-2020)

Time Period: Late 20th Century

Famous For: Turing Award Winner

Achievements: Frances Allen's work focused on compiler optimization, which greatly improved the efficiency and performance of computer programs. She was instrumental in advancing the field of automatic program parallelization, which allows programs to execute faster by performing several calculations simultaneously. Allen's pioneering research in optimization and parallelization laid the foundation for modern high-performance computing.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Karen Sparck-Jones (1935-2007)

Time Period: Late 20th Century

Famous For: Pioneer in Information Science

Achievements: Karen Sparck-Jones was a leading figure in information science, known for developing the Inverse Document Frequency (IDF). This weighting factor, which assesses a word's importance in a document, is now a standard component in web search engines, crucial for ranking a document’s relevance to a search query. Her influential work earned her the Lovelace Medal in 2007.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Mary Wilkes (born 1937)

Time Period: Mid 20th Century

Famous For: First Home Computer User

Achievements: Mary Wilkes is recognized for her contributions as a computer programmer and logic designer, most notably for designing the software for the LINC, an early example of an interactive personal computer. Her use of the LINC at home in 1965 marks her as the first ever home computer user. Her pioneering work in computing is acknowledged at The National Museum of Computing, Bletchley Park.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Adele Goldberg (born 1945)

Time Period: Late 20th Century

Famous For: Inspiration for GUI

Achievements: Adele Goldberg played a pivotal role in the development of Smalltalk-80, a programming language that significantly influenced Apple's first computer. Goldberg's contributions laid the foundation for graphical user interfaces (GUI), now a staple in computing systems worldwide, marking a transformative moment in the evolution of user-friendly computer interfaces.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Anita Borg (1949-2003)

Time Period: Late 20th to Early 21st Century

Famous For: Advocate for Women in Computing

Achievements: Anita Borg was a relentless advocate for women's participation in computing. She co-founded Systers, the first email network for women in tech, and the Grace Hopper Celebration of Women in Computing, a conference that highlights the contributions of women to computing. Her advocacy work has had a lasting impact on the visibility and participation of women in technology fields.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Radia Perlman (born 1951)

Time Period: Late 20th to 21st Century

Famous For: "Mother of the Internet"

Achievements: Known as the "Mother of the Internet," Radia Perlman's invention of the Spanning Tree Protocol (STP) algorithm is foundational for the modern internet. Her work has been critical in shaping how networks self-organize and move data, establishing the basic principles of internet traffic. Perlman continues to contribute to the field as a computer programmer and engineer.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Safra Catz (born 1961)

Time Period: Early 21st Century

Famous For: CEO of Oracle Corporation

Achievements: As the CEO of Oracle Corporation, Safra Catz has been a driving force in the company's growth and strategic direction. She played a pivotal role in Oracle's aggressive acquisition strategy, overseeing more than 130 acquisitions and significantly expanding the company's portfolio. Under her leadership, Oracle has strengthened its position as a leader in cloud computing and enterprise software solutions.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Susan Wojcicki (born 1968)

Time Period: Late 20th to 21st Century

Famous For: CEO of YouTube

Achievements: Susan Wojcicki has been a key player in the growth of Google and YouTube. As Google's first marketing manager, she was instrumental in the development and launch of successful products like Google Images and Google Books. Wojcicki played a vital role in the acquisition of YouTube by Google and has since steered YouTube to become the world's most popular video-sharing platform. Under her leadership, YouTube has introduced significant innovations in digital video, advertising, and content creator ecosystems, making it a central figure in the digital media landscape.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Reshma Saujani (born 1975)

Time Period: Early 21st Century

Famous For: Founder of Girls Who Code

Achievements: Reshma Saujani founded Girls Who Code, a non-profit organization aimed at closing the gender gap in technology. Through this initiative, she has empowered a generation of young women to pursue careers in tech and engineering. Girls Who Code has reached hundreds of thousands of girls, teaching them computing skills like programming, robotics, and web design, and fundamentally changing the narrative around women in tech.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Whitney Wolfe Herd (born 1989)

Time Period: Early 21st Century

Famous For: Founder and CEO of Bumble

Achievements: Whitney Wolfe Herd founded Bumble, a dating app that empowers women by allowing them to make the first move. Her innovative approach to online dating has challenged traditional norms and dynamics in dating culture. Wolfe Herd became the youngest woman to take a company public in the US with Bumble's IPO. Her leadership and vision have made Bumble a significant player in the social networking and dating app industry, promoting values of respect and empowerment.
